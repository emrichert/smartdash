About this dashboard

This dashboard visualizes county level COVID -19 case rates across the United States in 2020. The choropleth map displays cases per 1,000 adults, normalizing data for comparison across counties regardless of population size. Darker shades represent higher case rates.

The gauge updates when a county is selected, highlighting that county’s case rate relative to the full national range. This provides quick contextual reference of relative magnitude without requiring users to interpret the legend alone.

The scatterplot compares adult population size and COVID-19 case rates for all counties. Each point represents a county. This view allows users to examine whether more populous counties experienced higher case rates, or whether rates varied independently of population size.

How to use

  • Hover over the map to view county details
  • Click a county to update the gauge and highlight its position in the scatterplot
  • Use zoom within the scatterplot to explore dense regions
Source: NYT, 2018 ACS 5-year
